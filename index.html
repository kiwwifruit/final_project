Do Stress Patterns During Exams Reveal Hidden Insights About Students' Performance?

Stress plays a significant role in how students perform during exams. This dynamic visualization facilitates the exploration of how physiological stress markers‚Äîsuch as body movement, sweat response, heart rate, and skin temperature‚Äîrelate to exam grades by examining individual features and their combinations. The dataset includes exam data from 10 students across three exams, with their stress markers tracked by a smartwatch-like device worn on their non-dominant hand throughout the exam period.

Section 2: How Do Physiological Factors Fluctuate Throughout Different Exams?

This visualization helps identify patterns in stress, focus, and performance trends across different exam sessions. Use the dropdown menus to select a student and a physiological feature, then observe how it changes over time during Midterm 1, Midterm 2, and the Final Exam.

Although exam durations were fixed, the physiological data varied in length, often extending beyond the actual exam period. To address this, we aligned the start time for all students within each exam while incorporating a 1-hour buffer period before and after the exam.

Section 3: How Do Multiple Factors Combine to Predict Exam Grades?

While each factor provides valuable information, real-life stress responses are complex. This section allows you to explore how combinations of physiological markers work together to predict a student‚Äôs exam grade.

The predictions are generated by a linear regression model trained on an aggregated dataset, where each data point represents the mean value of each physiological marker per student per exam (30 student-exam combinations in total). The model uses four selected physiological markers (ACC, EDA, HR, TEMP) as features, with the exam grade (percentage) as the label.
